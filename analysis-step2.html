Phase 1: Step 1:
Activity 1 - Identify the intended use and the characteristics related to data security and privacy

Intended Use


Each risk analysis begins with identifying and documenting a clear description of the MDS AI model and its intended use. The following questions are suggested to raise when describing the MDS AI model:

Questions to be asked


# Question
1 What clincal purpose(s) does the MDS AI model serve (diagnosis, treatment planning, prognosis, monitoring or another clinical performance)?
2 Does the AI model drive or significantly influence the clinical management of the patient?
3 Who are the intended users of the MDS AI model (Will it be used by healthcare prefessionals, patients or other stakeholders)?
4 What target population the MDS AI model designed for (Are there any specific inclusion/exclusion criteria related to demographics, medical conditions or other factors)?
5 Does the MDS AI model include mechanisms to detect errors such as domain shift, out of distribution inputs, and missing or corrupted data?
6 In what clinical environment will the MDS AI model be deployed (Is it designed for use in hospitals, clinics, home settings or other healthcare environments)?
7 What autonomous functions, if any, does the MDS AI model provide? What is the level of that autonomy (E.x., decision support only, conditional automation, full automation)?
8 Does the MDS AI model have the ability to learn over time? If so, is there any potential impact to the intended use? What controls are in place to detect and correct for bias in new input data for models that learn over time?

Characteristics related to Data Security and Privacy


The following questions are suggested to raise when identifying the characteristics related to data security and privacy of the MDS AI model:

Questions to be asked


# Question
1Does the MDS AI model processes PII data?
2Are the training data, testing data, validation data, and resultant model data encrypted when at rest?
3Where is the data used by the model stored? and can it be altered after storage?
4Are backups created and stored elsewhere? Are backups encrypted? Are they encrypted prior to transmission? Is there a limit on how long backups are kept or on how many versions are kept?
5Is patient data scrubbed or obscured to remove personal information when not required?
6How is the integrity of the training data, testing data, validation data and resultant model data preserved during storage and transfer?
7Is the data encrypted when not at rest?
8Is encryption fixed or configurable? Is encryption based on a publicly recognised standard (E.g., AES 256) (recommended) or on a home-grown solution? How are keys managed?
9Are you training from user-supplied inputs (e.g., direct clinical data inputs from clinicians, technicians etc...,manual user entries such as patient demographics, symptoms or severiety scales) What kind of input validation/sanitisation techniques are used?
10Do portions of the data have different levels of sensitivity (PII/non-PII)? Is all data encrypted, or just PII? [Sensitive data - Personal data revealing the aspects such as biometric data for unique identification and health data of an individual (According to Article 9-GDPR)]
11What kinds of non-PII or personal data does the MDS AI model process (e.g., diagnostic data or therapy parameters)?
12Are controls in place to ensure the appropriate confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity of the data before and after transfer?
13If an adverse event occurs, can the data be recovered or restored?
14What controls are in place to ensure resilience against adversarial/poisoned inputs?
15How do you recover from adversarial contamination of your training data? (i) Can you isolate/quarantine adversarial content and re-train impacted models? (ii) Can you roll back/recover to a model of a prior version for re-training?
16What authentication and authorisation mechanisms are in place to ensure only authorised users can interact with the AI model and its data?
17How are credentials managed? Are credentials updated on a regular basis? How is credential expiration managed?
18Is there a system for logging and auditing access and modifications to the AI model and its data?
19Does your model return raw confidence scores or any other direct output which could be recorded and duplicated?
20Do you know from where the training data comes? Have you identified statistical norms (e.g., demographic distribution, population etc...) to understand what anomalies look like? What elements of your training data are vulnerable to outside influence? Who can contribute to the data sets you’re training from?
21What is the impact of your training data being recovered by attacking/inverting your model?
22What are interfaces to and interfaces from the AI model to the environment?
