Phase 1: Step 1:
Activity 3 - Estimation of risk(s) for each applicable threat and vulnerability combination

Risk is estimated by combining the likelihood that a threat will successfully exploit a vulnerability with the severity of the resulting impact.

Conducting risk estimation includes the following tasks:

  • Task 1: Determine Likelihood of occcurence (non-adversarial threats)/initiation (adversarial threats) of a threat event resulting in adverse impacts
  • Task 2: Determine Impact
  • Task 3: Determine Risk

Task 1: Determine Likelihood of Occurrence/Initiation

Likelihood of occurrence is a weighted factor based on a subjective analysis of the probability that a given threat is capable of exploiting a given vulnerability (or set of vulnerabilities). Note that the likelihood that a threat event will be initiated or will occur is assessed with respect to a specific time frame (e.g., the next six months, the next year, or the period until a specified milestone is reached).

The following likelihood factors need to be considered to assess the likelihood of occurrence of adversarial threats:

  • Adversary intent - The motivation, goals, willingness and opportunities of an attacker to carry out the threat event. When assigning likelihood values, the history of the attacker (e.g., using historical data, known threat actor capabilities, published exploitation rates) should also be considered.
  • Adversary capability - The attacker's technical skills, resources and overall ability to successfully carry out the threat
  • Adversary targeting - The targeted asset

  • For non-adversarial threat events, the likelihood of occurrence is estimated by: • Historical evidence or empirical data on the threat

During calculation, a likelihood level is assigned to each likelihood factor,and an overall likelihood level is then calculated by taking the average of all the likelihood factors.

Task 2: Determine Impact

Impact is defined as the extent to which a threat event might affect the MDS AI model, individuals, and the medical device software development organisation. When assessing impact, following impact factors need to be taken into consideration:

  1. Harm to operations - Any damage caused to the MDS AI model to produce incorrect results and lead to operational defects such as misdiagnoses, incorrect treatment recommendations, and cause any negative effects on clinical performance.
  2. Harm to assets - Any damage caused to the assets linked to the MDS AI model lifecycle.
  3. Harm to individuals - Any damage caused to the patients, including receiving incorrect treatments, delayed diagnoses, fatal medical errors that could cause harm or death of patients and loss of PII or damage to privacy of patients.
  4. Reputational harm - Any damage caused to the reputation or trust of the medical device software development organisation among different stakeholders, including patients, doctors, and hospitals.
  5. Financial loss - Any financial impact caused to the medical device software development organisation by product recalls, regulatory fines, and loss of revenue due to declining trust.

During the calculation, an impact level is assigned to each impact factor, and an overall impact level is then calculated by taking the average of all the impact factors.

(2) Semi-Quantitative

The semi-Quantitative risk determination operates as a two stage process

In the first stage,the qualitative risk level identified in (1) Qualitative risk determination is checked against the semi-quantitative assessment scales presented in Table below, which map each qualitative risk level to its associated score range on the 0–100 scale and corresponding bins value on the 0–10 scale. This table-lookup process identifies the score range and bins value within which the overall risk level falls, providing the numerical representation of the qualitative determination.

Assessment Scales – Overall Level of Risk (Semi-Quantitative)
Qualitative Values Semi-Quantitative Values Description
Very High 96-100 10 Very high risk means that a threat event could be expected to have multiple severe or catastrophic adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.
High 80-95 8 High risk means that a threat event could be expected to have severe or catastrophic adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.
Moderate 21-79 5 Moderate risk means that a threat event could be expected to have serious adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.
Low 5-20 2 Low risk means that a threat event could be expected to have limited adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.
Very Low 0-4 0 Very low risk means that a threat event could be expected to have negligible adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.

In the second stage, to aid the semi-quantitative assessment and produce an exact risk score within the identified score range, the following formula is applied using the bins values of the overall likelihood and overall impact established in Tasks 1 and 2:

Risk Score = Overall Likelihood × Overall Impact

This formula addresses an inherent limitation of the table-lookup process, since all threats carrying the same qualitative risk label receive the same score range and bins value regardless of the precise likelihood and impact values that produced that determination, the table-lookup alone cannot differentiate between threats within the same qualitative band. The formula produces an exact numerical risk score that locates each threat precisely within its qualitative band, enabling meaningful comparative prioritisation of risks for risk treatment purposes.
