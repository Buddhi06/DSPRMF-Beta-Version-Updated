Phase 2: Step 4:
Benefit Risk Analysis

A benefit-risk analysis should be performed for the residual risks that are judged unacceptable (identified in step 3) and for which further risk control is not practicable. The benefit-risk analysis is used to determine whether the residual risk is outweighed by the clinical benefit of the specific feature or design capability that gives rise to the risk.It is important to note that practicability of further risk reduction should be evaluated before initiating a benefit-risk analysis.

Practicability of further risk control measures can be evaluated by considering the following:

  1. Technical feasibility of the controls - Whether it is possible to implement the control with available technology and resources?
  2. Possibility of arising new risks - Whethere there is a possibility of introducing new risks, following the implementation of controls?
  3. Clinical performance assessment - Would the new control affect diagnosis decisions? Would it delay treatment decisions? Would it affect intended use of the MDS AI model?

To conduct benefit-risk analysis, the following activities should be followed:

  1. Determine Expected Clincal Benefit
  2. Determine Risk
  3. Compare Benefit-Risk
  4. Determine acceptability

(1) Determine Expected Clinical Benefit

The clinical benefit assessed in this activity is the direct clinical contribution of the specific feature giving rise to the risk to the intended clinical purpose and patient outcomes of the MDS AI model. Specifically, the degree to which the feature enables the MDS AI model to achieve its intended clinical purpose and deliver positive outcomes to patients.

The following factors need to be identified before calculating the benefit:

  • The feature giving risk to the risk - The asset-threat mapping established in Phase 1: Risk Assessment can be used as input for identifying the specific assets, or process componenet that introduces the risk.
  • The clinical role of the feature - Determine the significance of the feature in achieving the intended clinical purpose and direct contribution to patient outcomes

The expected clinical benefit is derived based on the Probability and Magnitude defined as follows:

  • Probability - the likelihood that the feature directly contributes to the positive patient outcomes via its role in the MDS AI model lifecycle.
  • Magnitude - the degree and significance of clinical contribution the feature makes to achieving the intended clinical purpose of the MDS AI model and to the to the expected positive patient outcomes.

Probability Assessment

The following assessment scales can be used to assess the probability.

Assessment Scales - Probability
Qualitative Values Semi-Quantitative Values Description
Very High 96–100 10 It is almost certain that the feature directly contributes to positive patient outcomes through its role in the MDS AI model lifecycle. The feature is a critical enabler of the model's intended clinical purpose
High 80–95 8 It is highly likely that the feature directly contributes to positive patient outcomes. The feature plays a significant role in enabling the MDS AI model to achieve its intended clinical purpose
Moderate 21–79 5 It is somewhat likely the feature directly contributes to positive patient outcomes. It plays a measurable but not critical role in the intended clinical purpose of the MDS AI model
Low 5–20 2 It is unlikely that the feature directly contributes to positive patient outcomes. Its role in the intended clinical purpose of the MDS AI model is limited
Very Low 0–4 0 It is highly unlikely that the feature directly contributes to positive patient outcomes. It does not play a significant role in the intended clinical purpose of the MDS AI model

Magnitude Assessment

The following assessment scales can be used to assess the magnitude. The Very High level should be reserved for features that are fully irreplaceable to the model's core clinical purpose.

Assessment Scales - Magnitude
Qualitative Values Semi-Quantitative Values Description
Very High 96–100 10 The feature makes a critical or transformative clinical contribution. It is essential to the MDS AI model achieving its intended clinical purpose and directly enables major positive impact on patient outcomes. This level should be reserved for features that are fully irreplaceable to the model's core clinical purpose
High 80–95 8 The feature makes a significant clinical contribution. It meaningfully enables the MDS AI model to achieve its intended clinical purpose and contributes clearly to improved patient outcomes, though comparable clinical purpose may be achievable through other means
Moderate 21–79 5 The feature makes a moderate clinical contribution. It contributes measurably to the MDS AI model's intended clinical purpose but is not essential and could be partially substituted
Low 5–20 2 The feature makes a limited clinical contribution. Its role in the MDS AI model's intended clinical purpose is minor and of marginal significance
Very Low 0–4 0 The feature makes minimal or negligible clinical contribution. It does not enable or enhance the MDS AI model's ability to achieve its intended medical purpose

(1) Benefit Value Calculation

After identifying the probability and magnitude qualitative values assign the corresponding bins values and calculate the benefit value using the formula below:

Benefit Value = Probability * Magnitude

Probability – Bins value corresponding to the qualitative probability level assigned using the scale above
Magnitude – Bins value corresponding to the qualitative magnitude level assigned using the scale above

(3) Compare benefit-risk


(1) Determine benefit-risk Ratio

Following the benefit value and risk values are determined, the benefit-risk ratio should be determined using the formula below:

Benefit-Risk Ratio = Benefit Value/Risk Value

📊 Benefit-risk Ratio Calculator

Use this calculator to determine the benefit-risk ratio using the benefit value and the risk value.



It should be noted that this approach assigns a value to each individual benefit and risk; it does not sum all risks or benefits to obtain a single value for each. This results in the identification of a pairwise comparison between each risk and each benefit. Although summarising benefits and risks to singular values would certainly present a clean summary with a singular benefit-risk ratio, such a method would lose the granularity that allows for an in-depth analysis of benefit and risk.

(4) Determine Acceptability

The acceptability of the benefit-risk comparison is determined by evaluating the ratio against Condition 1 and confirming through a mandatory expert multidisciplinary review (Condition 2).

Condition 1 — Benefit-Risk Ratio > 1

  • A benefit-risk ratio greater than 1 constitutes the primary numerical condition for acceptability, indicating that the feature-level clinical contribution benefit outweighs the residual security risk. A ratio below 1 indicates the residual risk outweighs the benefit.

Condition 2 — Expert Multidisciplinary Review

  • Relying solely on the numerical ratio is insufficient. Therefore, expert judgement by a multidisciplinary team is incorporated as a mandatory element of the acceptability determination.
  • The multidisciplinary review team comprising medical and clinical experts, cybersecurity specialists, regulatory professionals, and AI application experts must review the benefit-risk ratio within its complete clinical and cybersecurity context before a final determination of acceptable or not acceptable is reached. The team evaluates whether the ratio accurately reflects the clinical contribution of the feature relative to the residual security risk, and confirms the acceptability determination.

If analysis determined that the risks are outweighted by the benefits the manufacturer should determine what information should be communicated in labeling to manage this residual risk. Moreover, this needs to be communicated in such a way that it does not provide a blueprint for a potential attacker to exploit the non-mitigated vulnerability.

Example: Data theft threat on raw data at Stage1: Data Ingestion of the MDS AI model lifecycle

(1) Determine Expected Clinical Benefit

  • Feature giving rise to the risk: Collection of raw patient data creates a data asset that is valuable to attacker.

Probability Assessment

  • Raw data is the direct and primary source from which the model learns the clinically relevant patterns and determines its diagnostic accuracy which established a direct link between the feature and positive patient outcomes and making the contribution maximally specific. Therefore, this supports a “Very High” probability level where it is almost certain that collection of raw patient data directly contributes to positive patient outcomes through its role in the MDS AI model lifecycle. The qualitative level of “Very High” corresponds to a bins value of 10 as per the semi-quantitative assessment scales.

Magnitude Assessment

  • Collection of raw data directly helps in determining the accuracy of model’s decisions, clinical representativeness, and diagnostic validity of its outputs, highlighting core dimensions of the intended clinical purpose of the model. However, the magnitude is assessed as “High”, rather than Very High because there is a possibility that the model could use synthetic data instead of raw data, and achieve comparable clinical purpose. Therefore, the contribution does not meet the fully irreplaceable threshold required for Very High level.
  • The qualitative level of “High” corresponds to a bins value of 8 as per the semi-quantitative assessment scales.

Benefit Determination

  • Benefit Value = Probability * Magnitude
    Benefit Value = 10 * 80
    Benefit Value = 80

(2) Determine Risk

Let’s assume that the residual risk value determined in Step 3: Residual Risk Evaluation is 90 which represents a “High” residual risk.

(3) Compare Benefit-Risk

  • Benefit Risk Ratio = 80/90
    Benefit Risk Ratio = 0.88

(4) Determine Acceptability

  • The benefit risk ratio (0.88) is determined as <1, reflecting that the residual risk is outweighed by the benefit. However, to make the final decision of acceptability expert judgement should be taken into consideration as recommended.
